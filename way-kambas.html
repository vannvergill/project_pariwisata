The Wildlife

Endemic Species

Spanning over 1,300 square kilometers of swamp and lowland rainforest, Way Kambas National Park provides a critical sanctuary for Sumatra's megafauna. Meet the incredible species that call this park home.

Critically Endangered

Sumatran Elephant

Elephas maximus sumatranus

The park is famous for its Elephant Training Center. These gentle giants are smaller than their mainland cousins and are heavily protected due to rapid habitat loss.

Critically Endangered

Sumatran Rhino

Dicerorhinus sumatrensis

Way Kambas houses the Sumatran Rhino Sanctuary (SRS), a breeding facility dedicated to saving the smallest and most endangered rhinoceros species in the world.

Critically Endangered

Sumatran Tiger

Panthera tigris sumatrae

Elusive and magnificent, the park's dense forests offer one of the last remaining strongholds for the only surviving species of island tigers in Indonesia.

Endangered

Malayan Tapir

Tapirus indicus

Easily recognized by their black and white pattern, these shy herbivores roam the undergrowth of Way Kambas, playing a crucial role in seed dispersal.

Vulnerable

Malayan Sun Bear

Helarctos malayanus

The smallest of all bear species, the arboreal sun bear relies heavily on the dense canopy of the national park to find fruits, honey, and insects.

Endangered

Siamang

Symphalangus syndactylus

You can often hear the loud, booming calls of these tailless, black-furred gibbons echoing through the rainforest canopy during the early mornings.

Key Zones

Elephant Training Center (PLG)

Established to protect Sumatran Elephants, this center focuses on conservation, training, and mitigating human-elephant conflicts. Visitors can learn about the mahouts and interact responsibly with these gentle giants.

Learn More

Sumatran Rhino Sanctuary (SRS)

A world-renowned facility dedicated to the breeding and intensive conservation of the highly endangered Sumatran Rhino. While restricted to the general public to minimize stress on the rhinos, it serves as the beating heart of rhino survival efforts.

Learn More

Way Kanan Jungle Trek

For adventurous souls, the Way Kanan resort area offers pristine jungle trekking and river cruises. It is an excellent spot for birdwatching and spotting elusive primates swinging through the canopy.
